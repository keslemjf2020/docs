When you face a high-stakes strategic decision, querying a single agent gives you one perspective. Conclave gives you all of them at once. The `/conclave` command assembles a panel of AI agents — each representing a different functional role — to debate your question, critique each other's reasoning, identify hidden risks, and synthesize a single actionable recommendation. Every claim must be backed by evidence from your processed materials.

## What Is Conclave?

Conclave is Mega Brain's multi-agent deliberation system. It runs once per query through five structured phases, ensuring that every decision is pressure-tested before a recommendation is made.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Phase 0: Constitutional Foundation">
    Four governing principles are invoked before any agent speaks: **Empiricism** (decisions must cite data), **Pareto** (focus on the 20% with 80% of the impact), **Inversion** (ask what would make this fail), and **Antifragility** (prefer options that benefit from uncertainty).
  </Step>
  <Step title="Phase 1: Role Agent Debate">
    C-level agents — CRO, CFO, CMO, and COO — each present their position with supporting evidence and confidence scores. All claims must be traceable to `chunk_ids` from your materials.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Phase 2: Methodological Critic">
    A dedicated critic evaluates the *quality of reasoning*, not the positions themselves. It scores each argument across five dimensions and flags missing data, causal assumption errors, and unaddressed opportunity costs.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Phase 3: Devil's Advocate">
    The Devil's Advocate identifies the most fragile premise in the debate, names the highest-probability failure mode, and surfaces the most important alternative scenario that was ignored.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Phase 4: Synthesizer">
    Integrates all perspectives into a structured recommendation with a confidence score, phased action plan, residual risks, reversion criteria, and next steps with owners and deadlines.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Note>
  Conclave runs **once** per query — there are no loops or re-deliberations. If aggregate confidence falls below 60%, the session escalates to human decision and presents the options side by side instead of recommending one.
</Note>

## Running a Conclave Session

### Invoking Conclave

Pose your strategic question as a plain-language string:

```bash
/conclave "Should I change closer commission from 10% to 15%?"
```

Conclave automatically estimates the value at risk, selects the relevant role agents, and begins deliberation.

### High-Confidence Output (> 60%)

When agents reach sufficient consensus, you receive a full recommendation:

```
═══════════════════════════════════════════════
PHASE 4: SYNTHESIS
═══════════════════════════════════════════════

DECISION RECOMMENDED:
DO NOT raise commission to 15% across the board.

INSTEAD, implement 3-phase approach:

PHASE 1 (30 days): Diagnose turnover root cause
  • Conduct proper exit interviews (3rd party)
  • Survey current closers on satisfaction
  • Benchmark comp vs. market
  • Cost: $5K

PHASE 2 (60 days): Performance tier system
  • <60% close rate: 10% commission
  • 60-70% close rate: 12% commission
  • >70% close rate: 15% commission

PHASE 3 (90 days): Training investment
  • Script optimization workshop
  • Cost: $25K

CONFIDENCE: 78%

NEXT STEPS:
1. Approve Phase 1 diagnostic - Finance: CFO - By: Mar 20
2. Design tier structure draft - Sales: CRO - By: Mar 27
3. RFP for training vendor - Ops: COO - By: Apr 3

REVERSION CRITERIA:
IF after 90 days turnover ≥ 40% THEN escalate to CEO
IF tier system causes team conflict THEN simplify
```

### Low-Confidence Output (< 60%)

When data is insufficient or conflict is irresolvable, Conclave escalates to you instead of guessing:

```
═══════════════════════════════════════════════
    [CONCLAVE: DECISION INCONCLUSIVE]
═══════════════════════════════════════════════

⚠️ CONFIDENCE: 48% - BELOW THRESHOLD

TYPE OF UNCERTAINTY:
[✓] Data insufficient

OPTIONS FOR HUMAN DECISION:

OPTION A: Increase to 15% universally
  Trade-off: Higher retention vs. $180K cost
  Evidence: Exit interviews (weak)

OPTION B: Tiered commission structure
  Trade-off: Complexity vs. performance incentive
  Evidence: Unit economics (strong)

OPTION C: Investigate root cause first
  Trade-off: 30-day delay vs. better decision

⚠️ This case requires HUMAN DECISION.
   Recommended action: Gather missing data (Option C) first.
```

## The Four Constitutional Principles

Every Conclave session begins by invoking these principles. They constrain how agents reason throughout the deliberation.

<CardGroup cols={2}>
  <Card title="⚖️ Empiricism" icon="chart-bar">
    All claims must cite specific numbers and traceable `chunk_ids`. Anecdotes must be labeled as such. Opinions without data are flagged as violations.
  </Card>
  <Card title="📊 Pareto (80/20)" icon="chart-pie">
    Agents must identify the highest-leverage option and compare ROI across alternatives. "Do everything" solutions are penalized.
  </Card>
  <Card title="🔄 Inversion" icon="arrow-rotate-left">
    The Devil's Advocate must list failure modes. Each agent names at least one risk. The Synthesizer must address the top three risks explicitly.
  </Card>
  <Card title="💪 Antifragility" icon="shield">
    Agents identify which option improves under stress and uncertainty. Fragile solutions that require perfect conditions are ranked lower.
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

## Understanding the Agents

### Role Agents (Phase 1)

Each C-level agent brings a distinct functional lens to the debate:

| Agent | Focus | Bias |
|-------|-------|------|
| **CRO** | Revenue growth, sales performance | Actions that increase deals |
| **CFO** | Unit economics, profitability, risk | Conservative financial decisions |
| **CMO** | Positioning, demand generation | Brand and marketing investment |
| **COO** | Systems, scalability, execution | Operational improvements |

### Methodological Critic (Phase 2)

The Critic scores deliberation quality across five dimensions (0–20 each, 100 total):

- **Premises declared** — Did agents state their assumptions?
- **Evidence traceable** — Are `chunk_ids` properly cited?
- **Logic consistent** — Does the math hold?
- **Alternative scenarios** — Were other options considered?
- **Conflicts resolved** — Were disagreements addressed?

The Critic **does not** take a position, recommend an option, or inject new data. Its role is purely evaluative.

### Devil's Advocate (Phase 3)

The Devil's Advocate always delivers:

1. The most fragile premise in the debate, with reasoning
2. The main undiscussed risk (probability and impact estimated)
3. An alternative scenario that was ignored entirely
4. Reversal criteria — when to reconsider the decision

## When to Use Conclave

Conclave is best suited for:

- **Compensation and incentive structure changes** — high financial stakes, multiple stakeholders
- **Pricing and packaging decisions** — strategic tradeoffs with long-term impact
- **Hiring and headcount decisions** — capacity vs. skill vs. lead volume diagnostics
- **Operational process changes** — where one department's gain is another's friction
- **Significant budget allocations** — where opportunity cost matters

<Tip>
  For tactical questions like "How do I handle this objection?" or "What's a good cold call opener?", query a Cargo agent or Mind Clone directly instead. Conclave is designed for strategic decisions where multiple functional perspectives add value.
</Tip>
