Every expert thinks differently, but the structure of expert knowledge is surprisingly consistent: deep beliefs shape how they see the world, mental models define how they reason, heuristics guide fast decisions, frameworks systematize complex problems, and methodologies spell out exactly what to do. Mega Brain captures all five levels in a DNA Schema — a YAML document that encodes an expert's cognitive fingerprint with full source traceability. This schema is what powers every mind clone agent and gives cargo agents their synthesized judgment.

## The 5 Layers

<CardGroup cols={2}>
  <Card title="L1 — Philosophies" icon="brain">
    Core beliefs and worldview. The "why" behind every decision. These form the expert's identity and rarely change.
  </Card>
  <Card title="L2 — Mental Models" icon="diagram-project">
    Frameworks for understanding how systems work. These shape how the expert interprets reality and reasons through decisions.
  </Card>
  <Card title="L3 — Heuristics" icon="bolt">
    Practical rules and decision shortcuts. "If X, then Y" logic derived from experience — when to act, when to wait, when to pivot.
  </Card>
  <Card title="L4 — Frameworks" icon="layer-group">
    Structured multi-step systems for solving complex problems. Repeatable approaches with defined inputs and outputs.
  </Card>
  <Card title="L5 — Methodologies" icon="list-check">
    Detailed step-by-step execution plans with specific actions, sequences, and expected outcomes for each step.
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

<Tip>
  Think of the layers as moving from **why** (L1) → **how I see** (L2) → **when to act** (L3) → **how to structure it** (L4) → **exactly what to do** (L5). Together they give an agent both the wisdom to reason and the detail to execute.
</Tip>

## Layer Details

<Accordion title="L1 — Philosophies: Core beliefs that shape everything">
  Philosophies are the foundational convictions that an expert holds regardless of context. They're extracted from repeated, high-confidence signals across multiple sources.

  **Schema structure:**
  ```yaml
  filosofia:
    - id: FIL-001
      crenca: "Qualification is superior to persuasion in every sales context."
      implicacao: "Spend more time on discovery than on closing tactics."
      origem: "NEPQ Masterclass Session 1"
      confidence: 0.95
      sources: ["CG001", "CG003"]
  ```

  **Real examples:**

  | Expert | Philosophy | Confidence |
  |---|---|---|
  | Alex Hormozi | "Price is a consequence of value" | 0.95 |
  | Alex Hormozi | "Scale before you optimize" | 0.87 |
  | Cole Gordon | "Speed beats perfection in lead response" | 0.92 |
  | Cole Gordon | "Qualification is superior to persuasion" | 0.89 |
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="L2 — Mental Models: Frameworks for understanding reality">
  Mental models describe how an expert conceptualizes the systems they operate in. They shape intuition and make complex decisions feel obvious.

  **Schema structure:**
  ```yaml
  modelos_mentais:
    - id: MM-001
      modelo: "Three-Level Alert System"
      descricao: "Classify situations: Normal (continue), Attention (monitor), Critical (alert)"
      aplicacao: "Used to decide when to interrupt a workflow"
      exemplo: |
        Normal: Task proceeding, no blockers
        Attention: Dependency detected, monitoring
        Critical: Error requiring immediate decision
      sources: ["JV001"]
  ```

  **Real examples:**

  | Expert | Mental Model | Application |
  |---|---|---|
  | Alex Hormozi | Value Equation: (Dream Outcome × Probability) / (Time × Effort) | Evaluate and improve offers |
  | Alex Hormozi | Product Ladder: Front End → Back End → High End | Structure offer portfolio |
  | JARVIS | Cognitive Opportunity Cost | Every question has a cost in attention |
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="L3 — Heuristics: Rapid decision rules and triggers">
  Heuristics are the fast rules that experts apply without needing to reason from first principles every time. They encode experience into reflexes.

  **Schema structure:**
  ```yaml
  heuristicas:
    - id: HEU-001
      regra: "When in doubt, ask. When certain, execute."
      gatilho: "Decisions with reversible vs irreversible impact"
      acao: |
        Reversible: Execute and inform
        Irreversible: Ask before acting
      sources: ["JV001", "JV002"]
  ```

  **Real examples:**

  | Expert | Heuristic | Context |
  |---|---|---|
  | Cole Gordon | "Respond to leads in less than 5 minutes" | Speed impacts 80% of conversion |
  | Cole Gordon | "Always anchor before revealing price" | Anchoring defines value perception |
  | JARVIS | "5 seconds of context saves 5 minutes of confusion" | Briefings and handoffs |
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="L4 — Frameworks: Structured approaches to complex problems">
  Frameworks are multi-step systems that give structure to complex, recurring challenges. They're reusable across contexts.

  **Schema structure:**
  ```yaml
  frameworks:
    - id: FW-001
      nome: "SCAN - Status, Context, Action, Next"
      proposito: "Structure status updates for clarity and speed"
      estrutura:
        - Status: "Where we are now"
        - Context: "How we got here"
        - Action: "What was done"
        - Next: "Next steps"
      sources: ["JV001"]
  ```

  **Real examples:**

  | Expert | Framework | Purpose |
  |---|---|---|
  | Cole Gordon | NEPQ | Neuro-Emotional Persuasion Questions for qualification |
  | Cole Gordon | SPIN Selling | Situation → Problem → Implication → Need |
  | JARVIS | SCAN | Status, Context, Action, Next (for briefings) |
  | JARVIS | DECIDE | Define, Explore, Consider, Identify, Develop, Execute |
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="L5 — Methodologies: Step-by-step execution playbooks">
  Methodologies are the most concrete layer — detailed procedures with specific actions, expected outputs at each step, and defined sequences.

  **Schema structure:**
  ```yaml
  metodologias:
    - id: MET-001
      nome: "Session Initialization"
      passos:
        - passo: 1
          acao: "Load JARVIS-STATE.json"
          output: "Global state in memory"
        - passo: 2
          acao: "Check last session"
          output: "Context of where we stopped"
        - passo: 3
          acao: "Greet appropriately"
          output: "Time-appropriate greeting"
        - passo: 4
          acao: "Present status summary"
          output: "SCAN of current project state"
        - passo: 5
          acao: "Offer next steps"
          output: "Suggested action options"
      sources: ["JV001"]
  ```

  **Real examples:**

  | Expert | Methodology | Detail |
  |---|---|---|
  | Alex Hormozi | 30-Day Onboarding Pipeline | Day 1–3 Welcome, Day 4–7 Quick Win, Day 8–14 Core, Day 15–30 Optimization |
  | JARVIS | Self-Improvement Loop | Detect → Analyze → Propose → Implement → Verify → Document |
</Accordion>

## DNA Extraction Process

The pipeline extracts DNA automatically during processing. Here is how it works from raw material to finished schema:

<Steps>
  <Step title="Insight Collection">
    Phase 4 of the pipeline extracts actionable insights from every content chunk, each carrying a confidence score and `chunk_id` reference.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Layer Classification">
    Each insight is classified into one of the 5 layers based on its abstraction level — belief, model, rule, framework, or procedure.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Confidence Scoring">
    Confidence scores (0.0–1.0) reflect the strength of evidence. Insights reinforced across multiple sources receive higher scores over time.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Source Tracing">
    Every DNA element is linked back to its originating chunks, preserving full lineage for verification.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Cross-Validation">
    The pipeline identifies contradictions (flagged for review), reinforcements (confidence increases), and consensus patterns across sources.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Schema Generation">
    All layers are compiled into a YAML DNA schema file for the expert, stored in `knowledge/dna/`.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Complete DNA Schema Structure

Here is what a finished DNA schema looks like for an expert:

```yaml
# DNA Cognitivo - Alex Hormozi
# Generated by Pipeline Jarvis v2.1

expert:
  name: "Alex Hormozi"
  id: "alex-hormozi"
  sources_count: 23
  extraction_date: "2026-01-15"

layers:
  L1_filosofias:
    - belief: "Price is a consequence of value"
      confidence: 0.95
      sources: ["AH001", "AH002"]
    - belief: "Scale before optimizing"
      confidence: 0.87
      sources: ["AH003"]

  L2_modelos_mentais:
    - model: "Value Equation"
      description: "(Dream Outcome × Probability) / (Time × Effort)"
      application: "Evaluate and improve offers"
      sources: ["AH001"]

  L3_heuristicas:
    - rule: "Never negotiate on price without anchoring value first"
      context: "Anchoring defines perception before the number lands"
      sources: ["AH002"]

  L4_frameworks:
    - name: "Offer Stack Formula"
      steps:
        - "Dream Outcome identification"
        - "Probability maximization"
        - "Time and effort minimization"
      sources: ["AH001", "AH003"]

  L5_metodologias:
    - name: "Lead Magnet Launch Sequence"
      duration: "7 days"
      steps:
        - "Day 1: Tease + Problem awareness"
        - "Day 2-3: Education + Social proof"
        - "Day 4-5: Offer reveal + Scarcity"
        - "Day 6-7: Last chance + Close"
      sources: ["AH003"]

metadata:
  version: "1.0.0"
  pipeline_version: "JARVIS v2.1"
  total_chunks_analyzed: 847
  total_insights_extracted: 156
```

## How DNA Powers Your Agents

Extracted DNA schemas feed directly into agent files, giving each agent its reasoning foundation:

<Tabs>
  <Tab title="Mind Clone Agents">
    Mind clones in `agents/minds/{expert}/DNA-CONFIG.yaml` reason using the expert's actual extracted DNA. When you ask a mind clone a question, it draws on their real philosophies, models, and frameworks — not generic AI responses.
  </Tab>
  <Tab title="Cargo Agents">
    Cargo agents synthesize DNA from multiple experts relevant to their role. For example, a Sales-Lead agent combines DNA from Cole Gordon, Alex Hormozi, and Jeremy Miner — giving it a blended, role-optimized cognitive profile.
  </Tab>
  <Tab title="System Agents">
    System agents like JARVIS carry hand-crafted DNA schemas that define their operational philosophy and decision-making framework, giving them consistent and predictable behavior across all sessions.
  </Tab>
</Tabs>

## Full Traceability

Every DNA element links all the way back to the original source file:

```text
Philosophy (FIL-001)
  ↓
Sources: ["CG003", "CG007"]
  ↓
Chunks: ["CG003-042", "CG007-018"]
  ↓
File: inbox/cole-gordon/nepq-masterclass-session-3.txt:1247
```

You can verify any belief or rule by jumping directly to the passage where the expert said it.

## How DNA Evolves Over Time

DNA schemas are not static. Each time you process new material from the same expert, the schema grows:

- **New beliefs** are added with source references
- **Contradictions** are flagged for your review
- **Reinforced entries** receive higher confidence scores
- **Descriptions** become more precise as more context accumulates
