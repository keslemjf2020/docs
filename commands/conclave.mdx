Some decisions are too consequential for a single perspective. `/conclave` assembles the relevant cargo agents from your knowledge base, has them debate a question using evidence drawn from your processed sources, submits the debate to a Methodological Critic and Devil's Advocate for scrutiny, and then produces a synthesised recommendation with a confidence score, residual risks, and clear next steps. If confidence falls below 60%, the session escalates to you rather than guessing.

<Note>
Conclave runs **once** per query. There are no re-run loops. If the session ends with low confidence, that result is intentional — it means the question genuinely requires a human decision.
</Note>

## Syntax

```bash
/conclave [QUESTION]
```

<ParamField body="QUESTION" type="string" required>
  The strategic question or decision you want the council to deliberate on.

  **Examples:**
  - `"Should we increase closer commission from 10% to 15%?"`
  - `"Should we hire 5 or 10 closers for Q2?"`
  - `"Should we raise pricing on the core offer?"`
</ParamField>

## When to Use Conclave

**Good use cases:**
- Decisions that affect money, structure, or operational risk
- Questions where multiple roles have valid but different perspectives
- Situations where your knowledge base holds relevant evidence
- Reversible decisions where you want structured reasoning before acting

**Poor use cases:**
- Tactical execution questions ("Which CRM field should I use?")
- Questions your knowledge base lacks data to answer
- Urgent decisions where you need an answer in under an hour
- Highly subjective questions with no measurable criteria

## How It Works

Conclave runs 4 phases in sequence:

```text
┌─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│                        CONCLAVE FLOW                            │
├─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┤
│  Phase 0: Constitutional Foundation    [Governing principles]   │
│  Phase 1: Multi-Agent Debate           [Cargo agents deliberate]│
│  Phase 2: Methodological Critic        [Process evaluation]     │
│  Phase 3: Devil's Advocate             [Challenge assumptions]  │
│  Phase 4: Synthesis                    [Final recommendation]   │
└─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘
```

### Phase 0: Constitutional Foundation

Before any agent speaks, four governing principles are established. These override agent opinions if there is any conflict.

<Tabs>
  <Tab title="Empiricism">
    All claims must cite chunk IDs. No opinions or unattributed assertions.

    ```text
    ✓ "Close rate drops 40% without qualification ^[CG003_089]"
    ✗ "Close rate probably drops without qualification"
    ```
  </Tab>
  <Tab title="Pareto (80/20)">
    Agents must identify the 20% of actions that generate 80% of results. The session avoids "busy work" solutions.
  </Tab>
  <Tab title="Inversion">
    Before recommending what to do, agents must ask what would cause failure — then design around those failure modes.
  </Tab>
  <Tab title="Antifragility">
    Options that benefit from volatility and stress are preferred over fragile, fixed-state alternatives.
  </Tab>
</Tabs>

**Governance hierarchy:** Constitution → Protocols → Agent Instructions. The Critic in Phase 2 flags any agent that violates these principles.

### Phase 1: Multi-Agent Debate

The appropriate cargo agents are selected based on the question domain, and each presents a position with supporting evidence.

For a commission question, the agents might be `[CRO, CFO, SALES-MANAGER, CLOSER]`. For a hiring decision, `[CRO, SALES-MANAGER, COO]`. Each agent states:
- Their **position** (yes, no, conditional)
- Their **reasoning**, citing chunk IDs
- **Risks and trade-offs** they see
- Their **confidence level** (0–100%)

### Phase 2: Methodological Critic

The Critic evaluates the **quality of the debate process**, not the merit of any position. It scores the session across five dimensions:

| Dimension | Max Points | What It Checks |
|---|---|---|
| Premises declared | 20 | Were assumptions clearly stated? |
| Evidence traceable | 20 | Do all claims have chunk IDs? |
| Logic consistent | 20 | Are there internal contradictions? |
| Alternatives considered | 20 | Were other options explored? |
| Conflicts resolved | 20 | Were tensions between agents addressed? |

The Critic also identifies specific gaps — missing scenarios, uncited claims, unresolved tensions — that the Synthesizer must address.

### Phase 3: Devil's Advocate

The Devil's Advocate actively tries to break the emerging conclusion. It identifies:

- The **most fragile premise** in the debate
- The **main risk** the agents did not discuss
- A **12-month regret scenario** — what could go catastrophically wrong
- An **ignored alternative** that deserves consideration

This phase surfaces the blind spots that consensus-building tends to obscure.

### Phase 4: Synthesis

The Synthesizer integrates feedback from the Critic and Devil's Advocate into a final recommendation. The output includes:

- A **clear decision** — yes, no, or conditional
- A **confidence score** (0–100%)
- **Modifications** from the initial proposal based on the critical review
- **Residual risks** with named owners and mitigations
- **Next steps** with specific owners, actions, and deadlines
- **Reversal criteria** — the conditions under which you should pause or undo the decision

**Example synthesis output (abridged):**

```text
DECISION: YES - Increase commission with safeguards

CONFIDENCE: 78%

REVISED STRUCTURE:
  TIER 1: 12% base commission
  TIER 2: +3% accelerator at 80% quota
  TIER 3: +2% additional at 100% quota with NPS > 40

REVERSAL CRITERIA:
  Pause or revert if ANY of:
    • Churn rate increases > 5% absolute within 6 months
    • NPS drops below 35
    • Cost as % of revenue exceeds 18%

NEXT STEPS:
  1. Draft compensation policy — CRO + CFO — end of week
  2. Model 3 revenue scenarios — CFO — 5 days
  3. Socialise with top 3 closers — Sales-Manager — 1 week
```

## Low Confidence Outcome

If the final confidence score falls below 60%, Conclave does **not** make a recommendation. Instead, it presents the options it evaluated and explains what additional data would raise confidence:

```text
⚠️  CONFIDENCE: 52% — BELOW THRESHOLD OF 60%

TYPE OF UNCERTAINTY: Insufficient data

OPTIONS FOR HUMAN DECISION:

OPTION A: Increase to 15% immediately
  Trade-off: Speed vs risk | Confidence from: CRO, Sales-Manager

OPTION B: Phased approach (12% → 15% over 2 quarters)
  Trade-off: Safety vs market competitiveness | Confidence from: CFO

OPTION D: Gather more data (2-week research sprint)
  What's needed:
    • Survey top 10 closers on retention drivers
    • Benchmark 5 competitors' comp packages

⚠️  This case requires a HUMAN DECISION.
```

## Agent Roles

| Agent | Role |
|---|---|
| **Cargo Agents** (CRO, CFO, etc.) | Bring domain expertise and cite evidence from your knowledge base |
| **Methodological Critic** | Evaluates process quality — ensures rigor, not just agreement |
| **Devil's Advocate** | Challenges assumptions and surfaces hidden risks |
| **Synthesizer** | Integrates all feedback into a final actionable recommendation |

## Key Principles

1. **Evidence-based** — every claim must cite a chunk ID or it can be rejected
2. **Constitutional governance** — the four founding principles override any agent's opinion
3. **One-pass rule** — Conclave runs once; no loops; inconclusive results escalate to human
4. **Process vs. merit** — the Critic evaluates *how* agents reasoned, not *what* they concluded
5. **Adversarial testing** — the Devil's Advocate actively tries to break the conclusion before it reaches synthesis

## Troubleshooting

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title='"No relevant agents found"'>
    The question doesn't map to any available cargo agents. Check your `agents/cargo/` directory and ensure that agents exist for the relevant domain. Marketing questions, for example, require a CMO agent.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title='"Insufficient evidence"'>
    Your knowledge base doesn't have enough processed material to support the question. Ingest and process relevant expert content first, then retry:

    ```bash
    /ingest expert-video.mp4
    /process-jarvis "inbox/EXPERT/video.txt"
    /conclave "your question"
    ```
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title='"Confidence always below 60%"'>
    Persistent low confidence means the question is genuinely ambiguous, critical data is missing, or the question falls outside your agents' domain of expertise. In all three cases, a human decision is the correct outcome — not more deliberation.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>
